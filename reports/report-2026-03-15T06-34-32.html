Show the top 10 counties with the highest poverty rates in 2023. Include a bar chart and a table with each county's poverty rate, SNAP participation, and median income.

The top 10 counties with the highest poverty rates in 2023 are dominated by Puerto Rico municipalities. Mayagüez Municipio leads with a 53.9% poverty rate, followed by Ponce Municipio at 50.4%. McKinley County, New Mexico (38.2%) and Apache County, Arizona (29.8%) are the only mainland US counties in the top 10. Key findings: - Poverty rates range from 29.8% to 53.9% - SNAP participation is high across all counties (32.6% to 53.9%) - Median household incomes are notably low, ranging from $16,836 (Mayagüez) to $40,539 (Apache County) - Puerto Rico municipalities show the strongest correlation between high poverty and low median income
